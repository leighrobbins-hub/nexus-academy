Where DNA lives — and how cells copy it

Every cell in your body (almost) carries a full copy of your DNA. That DNA is packaged into chromosomes inside the nucleus. When cells divide, the chromosome story splits in two different ways—one for growth, one for making sperm and eggs.

Location

Nucleus: the cell’s library

In eukaryotic cells (like yours), DNA is stored in the nucleus, surrounded by a membrane. The rest of the cell does the “work” — but the nucleus holds the master instructions.

Packaging

Chromosomes come in pairs

Humans have 46 chromosomes in most body cells: 23 pairs. One chromosome in each pair came from your mother; the other from your father. They carry matching kinds of genes—though the alleles may differ.

The 23rd pair is special: sex chromosomes (typically XX or XY) help determine many sex-linked traits—not the only factor in identity, but important in genetics class.
Homologous pair — same genes, possibly different alleles.

Body cells

Mitosis — copy for growth & repair

Mitosis divides one cell into two genetically identical daughter cells (same chromosome number). It’s how you grow, heal a cut, or replace skin. Before mitosis, DNA replicates so each chromosome has two sister chromatids—then they split apart.

Sex cells

Meiosis — half the DNA for sperm & eggs

Meiosis makes gametes (sperm or egg) with only 23 chromosomes—half of a normal body cell. When sperm and egg fuse at fertilization, you’re back to 46—one complete genome for the new organism.

Without meiosis halving the number, each generation would double chromosomes forever. Meiosis also shuffles alleles (crossing over and random assortment), which helps explain variation in siblings.

Review

Flashcards

What is a chromosome?

A package of DNA + proteins that carries many genes—visible under a microscope when the cell divides.

Mitosis vs meiosis in one sentence?

Mitosis: two identical body cells. Meiosis: four genetically different gametes with half the chromosomes.

Why do egg and sperm have half the chromosomes?

So when they combine, the zygote gets a full set—one copy from each parent.

Check yourself

Quick quiz

1. In typical human body cells, chromosomes usually come in…

23 pairs — 46 total in diploid body cells.

2. Which process makes genetically identical body cells for growth?

Mitosis produces two nuclei/cells with the same chromosome set as the parent (after replication).

3. Human gametes (sperm or egg) typically have how many chromosomes?

Haploid number n = 23; fertilization restores 2n = 46.

4. Most of your DNA is stored in the…

Nuclear DNA (plus a small amount of mitochondrial DNA).
