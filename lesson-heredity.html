Mission 1 · Genes & alleles

Building blocks

Genes, alleles, and chromosomes

A gene is a stretch of DNA that often codes for a protein or trait. You inherit two copies of most genes—one from each parent. Each version is an allele.

Together, your alleles make up your genotype. What you show is your phenotype—genes plus environment.

Review

Flashcards

What is an allele?

A version of a gene—like different spellings of the same instruction.

What is a genotype?

The combination of alleles you carry for a gene (e.g. Tt).

What is a phenotype?

The observable trait—what we can measure or see.
